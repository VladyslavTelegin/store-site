The Problem

10,000 steps was a marketing slogan.

In 1965, a Japanese company sold a pedometer called "manpo-kei" -- literally "10,000 step meter." The number was chosen because the kanji character for 10,000 looks like a person walking. That is the entire scientific basis.

Every step counting app you have ever used is built on this fiction. Apple Health shows you a number. Pedometer++ shows you a number. They all imply the same thing: more is better, and you are not doing enough.

The research tells a different story. Health benefits from walking plateau well below 10,000 steps for most adults. What matters is consistency relative to your own pattern, not an arbitrary population target.

And context matters enormously. 5,000 steps after a hard gym session is not the same as 5,000 steps on a rest day. 6,000 steps on poor sleep is not the same as 6,000 steps fully rested. Yet every other app treats them identically.

stepiq does not count your steps. Your phone already does that. stepiq takes that number and gives it meaning, adjusted for the context of your actual day.

On Purpose

What we deliberately left out

Every feature below exists in competing apps. We removed each one intentionally, because they cause more harm than good.

×
Step Goals
Goals create failure. If you set 10,000 and walk 9,800, every app tells you that you failed. stepiq has no goals to fail at. Your baseline adapts to you, not the other way around.
×
Streaks
Streaks punish rest. Miss one day and your 47-day streak is gone. Streaks make you walk when you should recover, just to protect an imaginary number. stepiq never counts consecutive days.
×
Rings & Challenges
Gamification hijacks your dopamine system to create compulsive behavior. Closing rings becomes the goal, not your health. stepiq is a tool, not a game. It gives you information, not rewards.
×
Leaderboards
Comparing your steps to others is meaningless. A 25-year-old marathon runner and a 60-year-old recovering from surgery have different needs. Your health is not a competition.
×
Badges & Achievements
Virtual trophies are a manipulation tactic. They make you seek external validation for something that should be intrinsically motivated. stepiq offers no rewards, just honest assessments.
×
Push Notifications
No "You haven't moved in a while!" alerts. No "Close your rings!" at 10 PM. stepiq never interrupts your day. You check it when you want to, and it has an answer waiting.

Questions

Frequently asked

But I already see steps in Apple Health.
You do. And Apple Health does a great job counting them. What it does not do is tell you what that number means for you, today, given your workout this morning, your poor sleep last night, and the fact that it is only 3 PM. stepiq adds context to the count, not a second count.
What if I do not have much step history?
stepiq starts with a conservative default baseline of 6,000 steps. As it collects your data over 7-14 days, it builds a personal baseline using your actual patterns. No setup required.
How does the workout multiplier work?
When stepiq detects a workout from HealthKit (gym, running, cycling, yoga, etc.), it applies a ×0.6 multiplier to your step threshold. If your baseline is 6,200, your effective threshold after a workout becomes 3,720. Because you already moved.
Does it send push notifications?
No. Never. stepiq will never interrupt you. You check it when you want to, and it has a calm answer waiting. No "close your rings" at bedtime. No "you haven't moved" guilt trips. You are in control.
Why the median instead of the average?
If you walk 6,000 steps for six days and then hike 25,000 steps on Saturday, a simple average would set your baseline at ~8,700. The median stays at 6,000, which is your actual normal. One outlier day should not define your expectations for the next week.
Is my data sent anywhere?
No. 100% of stepiq's logic runs on your device. It reads from HealthKit on your iPhone and processes everything locally. No server, no account, no analytics, no tracking. Your step data stays on your phone.
What about the Apple Watch?
Pro includes Apple Watch complications that show your current state on your watch face. Tap to see the full context. The complication is designed to be glanceable and calm: a single word, a single color.
Can I change the 10,000 step goal?
There is no goal to change. stepiq does not have step goals. It has a personal baseline that it calculates from your own data. You cannot set it, override it, or gamify it. That is the point.
